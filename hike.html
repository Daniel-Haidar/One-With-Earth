HIKING

Hiking is a long, vigorous walk, usually on trails or footpaths in the countryside. Walking for pleasure developed in Europe during the eighteenth century. Ever since then, people have taken to this hobby, and have developed it into it's own form of meditation, where one can calm their mind by just walking.

Wether you want to hike in your local park, or in the depth of a the forest, hiking has huge benefits for your health, and it is recommended by halth experts as it helps both body and mind. it's udeniably something that more people should start, and we here at One With Earth can help get you ready to start in any way you prefer.


HIKING GEAR


Being well equipped on a hiking trail could go a long way in terms of how much you'd enjoyment you get from your hike, from essentials to quality of life so here's a checklist to make sure you're well prepared.

BACKPACK

You don't need much bells and whistles when it comes to your Hiking Pack,it just has to acomadate carrying all your equipment in it (or hanging off the side of it).
TREKKING POLES (optional)

These are really useful for making traversal of any terrain easier and less tiring, making them something to consider bringing along on your hike.
WEATHER-APPROPRIATE CLOTHING

You need to check the location's weather to see what level of heating, and in some cases sweat absorption, is needed for your hike. You should also pack more clothes than the bare minimum needed, it will come in handy if something unexpected occurs.
HIKING BOOTS/SHOES

These will bare the brunt force of the rough terrain, having a good pair of hiking footware will make for an easier, and more comfortable walk.
FOOD AND REFRESHMENTS

Be sure to pack enough food and water, enough for your trip as well as an entire day's worth o food as extra. Some things to consider are MREs (Meal Ready to Eat) and trail mixes of nuts and raisins, as both have a long shelflife and don't spoil, as well as being highly nutritious and will give you the some much needed energy.
NAVIGATION TOOLS

A map, a compass, and an optional SATCOM (sattelite communicator) are other essential things, a lot of amateur and experienced hikers have gotten lost in the woods and higly regretted not having something to get a lay of the land
FIRST AID KIT

It should be no surprise to anyone that this is on the list, having a first aid kit ready could mean the difference between life or death, it is not soemthing anyone should go without
MULTITOOL (optional)

While this is also optional, it will come in handy if you want to cut, repair, or even start a fire, a multitool can be very useful and versatile and is recommended.
FLASHLIGHT

A flashlight is everyone's favourite night companion, and should the first thing on anyone's list when going out into the wild.
SUN PROTECTION

These come in all shapes and sizes, but the more common options are sunscreen and a wide brimmed hat which help in protecting your skin, and in preventing heatstroke.
SUNGLASSES (optional)

This comes down to personal preference, a pair of sunglasses helps you see better in the glaring sunlight, especially useful for those with sensitive eyes that quickly dry up.
CAMERA (optional)

What better pictures to post on social media than of your hiking trip. From vistas, to lush greenery, to larger than life majestic mountains, a camera captures it all.
