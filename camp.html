Camping

Camping

is an outdoor activity involving overnight stays away from home, either without shelter or using basic shelter such as a tent or a recreational vehicle. Typically participants leave developed areas to spend time outdoors in more natural ones in pursuit of activities providing them enjoyment or an educational experience. The night (or more) spent outdoors distinguishes camping from day-tripping, picnicking, and other similarly short-term recreational activities.

Camping describes a range of activities and approaches to outdoor accommodation. Survivalist and wild campers typically set off with as little as possible to get by. Other campers might use specialised camping gear designed to provide comfort, including their own power and heat sources as well as camping furniture. Camping may be combined with hiking, as in backpacking, and is often enjoyed in conjunction with other outdoor activities such as canoeing, climbing, fishing, and hunting. Fastpacking involves both running and camping.

Equipment

Camping Equipment

A list of essential as well as optional/seasonal equipment you may need on your camping trip.
As your safety is our number one priority,it is very important to us that you are well informed on the many tools and equipments you can bring along with you to ensure the best camping experience as well as your safety and well being in the event that an emergency occurs.

Essential Equipment

  • Tent
  • Hiking boots
  • Multi-tool/Knife
  • Camp chairs and tables
  • Sleeping bags
  • Axe or Hatchet
  • Flashlight or Lantern
  • Cooler
  • Sunscreen
  • Insect repellent

Emergency Equipment

  • First-Aid Kit
  • Maps
  • Water purification devices
  • Compass
  • Radio

Cold Weather Camping

  • Well-insulated heating pads
  • Leg gaiters
  • Warm hat
  • Tape
  • Fire Starter
  • Raincoat or Poncho
  • Tarp
  • Blankets
